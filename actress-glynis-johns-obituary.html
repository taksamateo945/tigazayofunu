Remembering Glynis Johns: A Hollywood Icon

Glynis Johns, the esteemed British actress known for her iconic role as Mrs. Banks in the beloved Disney film Mary Poppins, has left an indelible mark on Hollywood. With a career that spanned over eight decades, Johns captivated audiences with her talent and versatility. Her recent passing at the age of 100 has left a void in the entertainment industry, as she was truly one of the last remaining stars from Hollywood’s golden age.

Introduction

Glynis Johns, born in South Africa, peacefully passed away at an assisted living home in Los Angeles. Her manager, Mitch Clem, confirmed that she died from natural causes. Clem aptly described it as a sad day for Hollywood, as Johns was not only a remarkable actress but also a symbol of the bygone era of classic cinema.
