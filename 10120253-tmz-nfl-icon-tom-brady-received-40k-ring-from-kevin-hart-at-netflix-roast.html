The ring comedian Kevin Hart gifted to legendary NFL quarterback Tom Brady during his roast on Sunday night reportedly cost five figures to make.

According to TMZ Sports, the ring was created by Jason of Beverly Hills CEO Jason Arashaben, and it is worth $40,000.

Since Brady was a good sport absorbing verbal barbs from former New England Patriots head coach Bill Belichick, former teammates Julian Edelman, Rob Gronkowski and Drew Bledsoe and many others, Hart rewarded him with the gaudy piece of jewelry, which resembles one of his seven Super Bowl rings.

The six-carat ring features nearly 400 diamonds, rubies and sapphires, and its resemblance to a Super Bowl ring makes sense, as Arashaben created the Tampa Bay Buccaneers' Super Bowl rings a few years ago.

Brady won his seventh and final ring with the Bucs after winning his first six Super Bowls as a member of the Patriots dynasty.

The roast, which was entitled The Greatest Roast of All Time: Tom Brady, streamed live on Netflix and went viral due to the star power involved and the deeply personal jokes some of the roasters made at Brady's expense.

While many of the roasters were longtime friends of Brady, they didn't hesitate to poke fun at his divorce, the Deflategate scandal, his Patriots departure and much more.

Brady, 46, retired after the 2022 season as arguably the greatest quarterback and greatest overall player in NFL history. In addition to his seven Super Bowl wins, Brady was a 15-time Pro Bowler, three-time first-team All-Pro, three-time NFL MVP and five-time Super Bowl MVP.

Brady is also the NFL's all-time leader in passing yardage and passing touchdowns by virtue of his highly productive 23-year career with the Pats and Bucs.

While Brady is one of the most beloved athletes of all time, he hit some bumps along the way, and he took it in stride when the roasters brought up his shortcomings.
